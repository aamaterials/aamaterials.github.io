Previous Members

Peyman Z. Moghadam Previous Senior Research Assistant

Peyman was a Research Associate in our group. His expertise is in the combination of high-throughput molecular simulations and adsorption measurements for studying adsorption phenomena in advanced materials, in particular self-assembled porous crystals. Peyman is now a Lecturer in the Department of Chemical and Biological Engineering at the University of Sheffield.
Michelle Teplensky Previous PhD Candidate

Michelle obtained her PhD in our group in 2018. Her project focused on the application of MOFs to drug and gene delivery. She is now a postdoctoral researcher at Northwestern University.

Tian Tian Previous PhD candidate

Tian obtained his PhD in the group. His research involved synthesising monolithic MOFs for gas storage and separation. Tian is also a co-founder of Immaterial, a spin-out from the group. Tian is now a postdoctoral reseacher working in the Multifunctional Nanomaterials group in the Department of Chemical Engineering at Imperial College London.

Maryam Razmjooei Previous Visiting Student

Maryam Razmjooei was a PhD student at Shiraz University, Iran, specializing in Food Biotechnology. In 2022, she joined the research group as a visiting scholar, focusing on the utilization of metal-organic frameworks (MOFs), specifically PEGylated PCN-222, for the encapsulation of insulin as an oral delivery system. Her research explored nanoparticle behavior, including in vitro release in simulated gastrointestinal conditions and the cellular uptake of PEG@PCN-222@insulin using Caco-2 and HT29-MTX-E12 cell lines.

Yue Yang Previous PhD Candidate

Yue was a PhD student in the group. His research focused on the green synthesis of monolithic MOFs for energy-related applications.

Ali Al-Shakhs Previous PhD Candidate

Ali was a PhD student in the group. His research focused on the synthesis of MOF monoliths for Natural Gas storage and upgrade applications.

Andi Tao Previous PhD candidate

Andi obtained his PhD in the group in 2018. His research interest were the identification of optimal nanoporous MOF structures for gas separation using molecular simulation, as part of the BP International Centre for Advanced Materials. He studied Engineering Science at St Edmund Hall, Oxford.

Claudia Orenalla Previous PhD Candidate

Claudia Orellana obtained her PhD in the group. Her work focused on using MOFs for drug delivery applications in order to reduce the side effects and to enhance the efficacy of therapeutic agents. The main aim of her research project was to study MOFs as efficient drug delivery systems compared to free drugs using in vitro experiments on cancer cell lines.

Jacek Osinki Previous MPhil Student

Jacek was an MPhil student in the group. Jacek worked on molecular simulation of MOFs for applications in gas separations relevant to the petrochemical industry as part of the BP International Centre for Advanced Materials.

Chun Man Chow Previous MPhil Student

Chun Man was an MPhil student in the group. His research involved using molecular simulation tools to analyse the stability of MOFs in adsorbed natural gas systems.
