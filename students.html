Current Research Students

Diana Vulpe PhD candidate

I am a final year PhD student working on the development on MOF composite materials and their application towards water treatment, focusing on removal of endocrine disrupting chemicals. Whilst in Cambridge, and not swamped in work, I enjoy running and reading.

Marta
Aragones-Anglada PhD Candidate

I am a 2nd year PhD student working on the computational study of porous materials for gas adsorption and storage applications. Moreover, I employ molecular-level simulations and other computational tools to help understand the particular behaviour of some materials and for the rational design of materials. Prior to starting my PhD, I obtained the MSc in Advanced Chemical Engineering course at Imperial College London. In my free time, I enjoy reading, sports, and arts.

Elise Siouve PhD Candidate

Elise is a 2nd year PhD student from the 2017 CDT cohort in sensor technologies. Elise’s research focuses on surface modification of MOFs for targeted drug delivery in cancer therapies. Her work is done in collaboration with MedImmune, a biotechnology company with strong expertise in antibody technologies. In her spare time, Elise enjoys travelling, meeting new people and playing volleyball.

Yunhui (Selina) Zhuang PhD candidate

Selina is a 1st year PhD student. Her research focuses on the delivery of biomacromolecules for therapy applications. In her spare time, she enjoys dancing salsa. She is also the president of the Graduates Researchers' Society in the department.

Aurélia Li PhD Candidate

I am a 2nd year PhD student. I work in collaboration with the Cambridge Crystallogphic Data Centre to provide useful tools for the MOF community.

Bethany Connolly PhD Candidate

Bethany is a 3rd year PhD student working in the departments of Chemistry and Chemical Engineering. She is developing new monolithic MOFs and NP@MOF composite materials for high density gas-fuel storage.

Wilson Wang MPhil in Biotechnology

I am an MPhil in Biotech student. My research focuses on functionalisation of the surface of MOFs for targeted drug delivery capability and improved stability in the human body. In my free time, I enjoy playing tennis and doing improvisational and stand-up comedy.

Johannes Osterrieth PhD Candidate

Joshua Mehta PhD Candidate

New synergies between magnetic hyperthermia and drug delivery for cancer therapy. Funded by the Cambridge Cancer Centre (CRUK), this project looks at the incorporation of magnetically heatable nanoparticles into porous metal-organic frameworks for triggerable therapeutic release.
